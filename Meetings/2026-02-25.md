Explanation:
First, the setup requires user specified set of REGIONs, YEARs, SEASONs, DAYTYPEs, and DAILYTIMEBRACKETs. YEARs are ordered. The DAILYTIMEBRACKETs completely divide all 24 hours of a representative day. These are ordered (think 00:00 - 06:00, 06:00 - 12:00, etc). SEASONs completely divide all days in a year. These are also ordered (think Q1, Q2, etc). A TIMESLICE is SEASON x DAYTYPE x DAILYTIMEBRACKET. So TIMESLICEs are in a way semi-ordered (i.e. TIMESLICEs in SEASON1 before SEASON2, etc from the perspective of SEASONs and TIMESLICE with TIMEBRACKET1 before TIMEBRACKET2, etc from the perspective of DAILYTIMEBRACKETs). I think this is the point I struggled to communicate yesterday.  
  
DAYTYPEs are repeated aggregations of DAYs in a SEASON (i.e. think Weekday/Weekend or, more generally, Normal/Abnormal). A model user is _encouraged_ to come-up with DAYTYPE distinctions that show greatest divergence in trend/pattern/representation within a representative YEAR. One could also make DAYTYPEs sequential (e.g. Week1Weekdays, Week1Weekend, Week2Weekdays, Week2Weekend, etc) to fully represent each SEASON. I enforce this when translating from PyPSA to OSeMOSYS and have designed (in my opinion) a comprehensive test suite to ensure as much as possible that the translation does not create unintended model artifacts.  
  
So to recap, the spatial dimension is REGION and the temporal dimension is YEAR x TIMESLICE. Users also specify a set of STORAGE options with various performance and economic attributes at each spatio-temporal resolution.  
  
Storage works through a cascading constraint system:  
- Due to the semiordered-ness of TIMESLICEs, we can calculate Net Charge within a YEAR and within a DAY.  
- Similarly, we can calculate the state of charge at the start and end of each YEAR, SEASON, DAY by taking the previous and adding the net charge within that period.  
- StorageUpperLimit = AccumulatedNewStorageCapacity + ResidualStorageCapacity (i.e. capacity constraint)  
- operational bounds dictate that storage must be within [StorageLowerLimit, StorageUpperLimit] at each point in time (i.e. each YEAR x TIMESLICE)  
  
The result is the minimum storage capacity needed to meet demands at minimum total system cost.
